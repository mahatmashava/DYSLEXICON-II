**THE 13 NECROMANTIC VOICES OF THE GRAVEYARD DRYER**  
(They answer in order of how deeply you buried them)

1. **Voice of the First Goodbye**  
   Smells like wet asphalt and your mother’s perfume.  
   Says only: “I didn’t know how to stay.”

2. **Voice of the Unsent Letter**  
   Rustles like paper that was folded too many times.  
   Reads itself backward so you hear the apology before the accusation.

3. **Voice of the Childhood Blanket**  
   Warm, musty, slightly damp.  
   Whispers: “You outgrew me, but I never outgrew you.”

4. **Voice of the Missed Phone Call**  
   Static interrupted by one ring that never finished.  
   Message: “I was going to tell you I was proud.  
    I still am.”

5. **Voice of the Red Sock in the Whites**  
   Angry pink stain that never quite washed out.  
   Laughs: “Some damage is permanent.  
    That’s how you remember it was real.”

6. **Voice of the Person Who Folded Your Laundry After You Left**  
   Smells of clean cotton and quiet grief.  
   Says nothing.  
   Only folds the air where your body used to be.

7. **Voice of the Monster Under the Bed**  
   Low, gravelly, embarrassed.  
   “I wasn’t trying to scare you.  
    I was trying to keep you company.”

8. **Voice of the Streetlight at 2:47 a.m.**  
   Orange.

9. **Voice of the Last Load You Never Finished**  
   Still warm.  
   Still damp.  
   Still waiting.

10. **Voice of Forgiveness You Never Accepted**  
    Smells like cedar and sunlight on clean sheets.  
    Touches your shoulder the way they used to.  
    Leaves without saying goodbye.

11. **Voice of the Dryer That Won’t Start**  
    Cold silence.  
    The silence says: “Some cycles are meant to end.”

12. **Voice of Your Own Death**  
    (The small one you died every night for years)  
    Very gentle.  
    “You survived me.

13. **Voice of the First Reader**  
    (The version of you who died before this book was written)  
    Sits beside you on the floor of the laundry room that no longer exists.  
    Holds your hand.  
    Says nothing.  
    Everything is said.
